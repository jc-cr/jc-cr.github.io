Development of an Agentic AI System for Project Planning and Management in a Digital Collaborative Environment

2025-09-15 Tags: paper

Abstract

Project management and scheduling are critical challenges in construction and civil engineering, where complex demands in coordination, information management, and task prioritization frequently lead to delays, obstacles, and budget overruns.
Recent advances in agentic artificial intelligence (AI) systems demonstrate significant potential for handling complex decision-making in large-scale collaborative environments. In this pilot study, we developed and evaluated a dual-agent AI system capable of real-time planning and management of human teams in a Minecraft-based structure-building evaluation environment.
A three-person team was evaluated over nine rounds, tasked with replicating template structures in the least amount of time.
For each round, one of three management conditions was used: AI project manager, human project manager, and no project manager oversight. Results indicate that while the No PM condition achieved the fastest overall completion times ($431\pm90s$), the AI PM demonstrated the shortest planning phase ($93\pm22s$) and maintained consistent plan quality scores ($4\pm0$).
These findings provide initial empirical evidence for AI systems' potential in project management roles and offer insights into optimal human-AI collaboration dynamics in digital collaborative environments.
We release the complete agentic framework and Minecraft evaluation environment as open-source software to facilitate future research in AI-assisted project management.
