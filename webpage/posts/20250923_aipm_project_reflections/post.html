AIPM Project Reflections

2025-09-23 Tags: penning

Context & Objectives

We recently completed an initial findings report for the AIPM project, a study to see if an agentic project manager could outperform humans for directing human workers.

Although we met the deadline for initial paper submissions to the CRC conference, there were many internal milestones that our team failed to meet as well as key targets from our originally drafted Project Requirements Doc.

What Went Well

  • Met paper submission deadline
  • Created a functional AI PM framework that received real-time Minecraft data
  • Completed a functional Build Mart replica for evaluating project management capabilities in Minecraft
  • Completed a pilot study to gather comparison data between different human management methods
  • Managed to adapt to asynchronous workflow

What Didn't Go Well

  • Began to fall behind around June (aligning with summer activities and travel)
  • Online live collaboration meetings were frequently not attended
  • Planned larger human study fell through due to scheduling issues and difficulty accessing UTSA training platform remotely
  • I ended up doing more low-level work than I expected, likely due to failure in my task delegation to other members

Key Lessons Learned

  • Although live collaboration was a good plan, should have also made better use of issues and a digital kanban board for clearer task assignment and planning. This would have helped to better communicate big picture to team
  • Documentation started to lag as development pace increased, this could be remedied by not overburdening individual contributors (IC)
  • Better experiment and testing protocols should have been made to make better use of testing time before live study

Actions for Next Time

  • Implement a digital kanban system (move to Gitlab if we can't on Github)
  • Make sure to actively update Concept Paper
  • Keep steady 60-80% workload for all developers. This varies by IC so ensure you know the IC's schedule and capabilities
