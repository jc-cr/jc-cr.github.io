Overall thoughts

  • Well organized but long conference, usually pretty tired by 1600 with all the interesting presentations.
  • This conference covers a breadth of topics. Some that interested me were the additive printing cases with clay and cement, the use of AI enabled robots, and some intelligent planning works.
  • I think what made the best paper impact is that it was done by a company, built off of previous research directly, had good thought process in the innovation, and gave some direct results from evaluation that incorporate real-world application. Seemed like a work that could be published in multiple venues as well like IROS.
  • But overall I think I still need to learn some more to make a better assessment. The conferences I've gone to so far in my career have just been figuring out what to do and how to make the most out of these experience; also I've just been getting a feel for what is being researched at the moment in this domain.
  • I liked Wednesday morning talk. Better metric for evaluating researchers than just published papers: "What is your impact?". Goes beyond publishing, it assess project you've worked on, communities you have helped, etc. So it's important to be able to have good metrics personally for being able to gauge what you've done and how you have helped the world beyond just publications.
