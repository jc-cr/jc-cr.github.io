Biography

Juan Carlos Cruz Rivera is a multifaceted engineer and artist from Austin, Texas, who began his creative journey as a rap artist during his final year of high school. After struggling to gain recognition in the music industry, he enlisted in the Texas Army National Guard as an Aircraft Electrician.

Following his military service, Mr. Cruz enrolled at the University of Texas at San Antonio, initially pursuing Microbiology driven by his fascination with the history of plagues and how microorganisms can profoundly impact humanity. His academic interests evolved as he discovered new passions, transitioning first to Mechanical Engineering due to his love of system design and collaborative projects, before ultimately finding his calling in Electrical Engineering. He graduated in 2023 with a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering with a concentration in Computer Engineering, complemented by a minor in Mathematics and a Certificate in Artificial Intelligence.

He completed his Master of Science in Advanced Manufacturing and Enterprise Engineering in 2025, focusing on automation, robotics, and Industry 4.0 applications. As a Research Assistant in the Automated and Intelligent Construction Lab under Dr. Jiannan Cai, he has contributed to multirobot autonomous infrastructure inspection projects, publishing conference papers at ASCE and ISARC conferences. His industry experience includes developing autonomous robot systems at Plus One Robotics, and he is currently completing an internship at the U.S. Air Force Research Laboratory, where he develops benchmarking methods for multimodal and language models in robotic applications.

His academic excellence has been recognized with the TRANS-IPIC UTC Outstanding Student of the Year award and the Dwight David Eisenhower Transportation Fellowship. In his free time, he enjoys riding his motorcycle, writing haikus, and mentoring robotics competition teams where he applies lean product development principles and shares the Toyota Way.
